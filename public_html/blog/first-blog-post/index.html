ECTN Online Application Process

Necessary Documents

To apply you will need to gather these shipping documents:

  • Bill of Lading
  • Commercial Invoice
  • Freight Invoice
  • Optional: Customs Declaration, Export Declaration or additional documents such as certificate of origin or packing list

Step-by-Step Application Process

Once the mandatory documents are obtained, you will begin the application process:

  1. Submit: Send AfricaCTN the required documents
  2. Draft ECTN and Invoice: Receive the draft copy and invoice directly to your invoice
  3. Approve: Check and confirm all details of the draft ECTN matches your documents
  4. Pay: Make payment of the invoice so validation can begin
  5. Validated ECTN: Issuance of the final validated ECTN number / BESC ready for the importer and customs clearance with the respective customs authorities

Key Information

  • The ECTN can also be known as a BESC Certificate, BSC, Waiver, FERI, ARCCLA, CNCA, BIETC, CTN depending on the destination country / destination port
  • The following countries require the ECTN:
    • Angola, Benin, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, Democratic Republic of the Congo, Djibouti, Ghana, Guinea Bissau, Ivory Coast, Liberia, Madagascar, Mali, Niger, Republic of Congo, Republic of Guinea, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Togo, Yemen
  • AfricaCTN is an authorized agent for many of the countries that require it
  • Responsibility: It is the responsibility of the shipper or freight forwarder to begin the ECTN application
